1.2 Product Family Overview

Solid state file caching technology can eliminate I/O bottlenecks and boost server performance and scalability in transaction-intensive applications.

The Excellerator family of solid state storage systems from Solid Data Systems (formerly Database Excelleration Systems) comes with Fast/Wide or Ultra/Wide SCSI interfaces. Storage capacity ranges from 536MB to a maximum of 34GB on the 1000. The product line supports NT and all major versions of Unix. The Excellerator systems connect to existing systems and feature internal back-up disk drives and memory parity protection.

The family consists of the 600, 800, 1000 and the Ultra series: 600 Ultra, 800 Ultra, 1000 Ultra, and the 1U height e100 series.

1.3 Model Descriptions

Excellerator 600, 600 Ultra, and 800 Ultra Key Features
  • Provides high speed access to I/O intense Unix and NT data files
  • Enhances the performance of existing RAID and JBOD storage systems
  • Scales from 536MB up to 34Gb, depending on the model
  • System self-configures to number of array boards installed
  • Up to 15 systems can be configured on a single SCSI Wide bus
  • Incorporates a non-volatile system architecture
  • Redundant power supplies (800 and 1000 Ultra only) and a hot-swappable backup disk drive
  • Backup and restore data rate of 536MB/min
Excellerators are a family of self-contained solid state storage systems that use non-volatile SDRAM technology. The Excellerator Ultra family is designed for applications with a high rate of usage such as digital media, internet services, relational databases, and on-line transaction processing (OLTP). All four models have an industry-standard SCSI interface and support all major Unix and Microsoft NT platforms.

By utilizing SDRAM technology the Excellerator solid state storage system eliminates the mechanical delays associated with conventional magnetic disk drives. Users of "hot files" will notice data transfer rates over 90% faster when those hot files are moved onto either the 600 or the 800.

To overcome the volatility of SDRAM components, the Excellerator systems have a Data Retention System that includes an integrated battery backup, an onboard disk drive with a separate data path that is not dependent upon the host processor, automatic backup control logic, and a redundant array of independent chips (RAIC). If power is lost, the Data Retention System provides uninterrupted power to the SDRAM array. If the power does not come back on after a user-designated time period, the Excellerator will perform a back up of all data onto the internal disk drive. The 800 Ultra is suitable for mission-critical environments because it has redundant power supplies and a removable backup disk, which can be moved over to a standby system.

The Excellerator systems continuously monitor power conditions and will automatically switch over to the battery backup during outages. Two lead acid gel cell batteries can power the system for 1 to 2.5 hours. During normal operating conditions the systems have internal full time battery charging with automatic charge monitoring. The internal backup disk drive uses a dedicated internal Ultra SCSI port. The disk drive remains in a "standby" mode under normal system conditions. An automatic restore is performed once AC power returns.

Installation of an Excellerator system should require less than an hour because they incorporate an industry-standard SCSI interface. A Unix or NT server will see the Excellerator as just another disk drive. The systems do not require any special configuration of the operating system or of the database engine.

Excellerator 1000 Ultra Key Features
  • Sustains Ultra SCSI data transfer rates of 40MB/second in both sequential and random activity
  • Data access time of 14 microseconds is 1000 times faster than that of magnetic drives
  • Up to 34.24GB capacity per system
  • System self-configures to number of array boards installed
  • Up to 15 systems can be configured on a single SCSI Wide bus
  • Non-volatile system architecture
  • Redundant power supplies
  • Backup and restore data rate of 1GB/min
The Excellerator 1000 Ultra has all of the features and capabilities of the 600 Ultra and 800 Ultra with the addition of 11 more RAM slots.
Excellerator e100
The e100 height was designed for applications where rack space is a concern, including server farms and outsourcing facilities.

Remote Monitor
The Remote Monitoring and Reporting (RMR) feature is an option on the 800 and 1000 chassis'. RMR allows Ethernet LAN connectivity to monitor failures or out of tolerance conditions of:
  • 5 and 12 volt power converters and power supplies
  • Battery charging voltage
  • AC line input voltage
  • Backup drive status
  • Temperature
This information is viewable from either
  • SNMP alert generated for out of tolerance conditions
  • Web-based
RMR is a $2000 optional feature for the 800 and 1000. RMR may also be an 'in-factory' upgrade for existing systems. RMR consists of a Remote Monitor Board (attaches to chassis), and a Remote Monitor Card (attaches to N+1 Power Supply).

Note: As of 2002, it appears that RMR is now built into each SDS chassis, including the e100, 800, 800N, and 1000.

4.6.1 Startup/Shutdown Procedures
NOTE: Since the Excellerator uses DRAM technology there are specific startup/shutdown procedures to follow to ensure data integrity is maintained. These procedures are detailed below. They are also available online at:
http://www.soliddata.com/support/manuals/800ops.pdf

Following are the startup and shutdown procedures for Solid Data Systems drives. 

Review of Front Panel Functions
Figure 2. SDS 600 or 800 Front Panel LEDs

KEY Switch (Front panel)

The front panel key switch is used to prevent the SHUTDOWN and RESTART switches from being activated by unauthorized personnel. 

  • Vertical position: When the key switch is in the vertical (lock) position, the SHUTDOWN and RESTART switches are deactivated. 
  • Horizontal position: When the key switch is in the horizontal position, the SHUTDOWN and RESTART switches are activated. 
The position of the front panel key switch does not prevent the automatic saving of memory contents during power line failure. 

SHUTDOWN Switch

The SHUTDOWN switch, located on the front panel of the Excellerator, initiates the shutdown sequence. Pressing the SHUTDOWN switch will copy the entire contents of memory to the internal backup device. When the backup operation has completed, DC power to the drive can be turned off using the rear panel Master DC Power Key Switch.

The shutdown process moves the data on a block-by-block basis to locations in the backup device. All of the data is saved exactly as it existed in memory when the shutdown sequence was initiated. Saving the contents of the memory array to the backup device does not affect the contents of the memory array. During the shutdown process, the Excellerator is off-line to the host.

RESTART Switch

The RESTART switch, located on the front panel of the Excellerator, allows the Excellerator to be reactivated after a shutdown sequence without turning off power via the rear panel Master DC Power Switch. Pressing the RESTART switch after a successful shutdown sequence will immediately return the unit to the ready state with the memory array contents intact. When a restart occurs, the SHUTDOWN COMPLETE indicator will extinguish and the SYSTEM READY lamp will illuminate indicating that the Solid Data Excellerator has returned to an on-line condition.

The restart process has no effect on data stored in the memory arrays. Stored data is left exactly as it was prior to the shutdown sequence being initiated. 

Review of Rear Panel Functions
Figure 3. SDS 600 or 800 Rear Panel

 Master DC Power Switch

The Master DC POWER key switch is located on the rear panel of the Solid Data Excellerator. This switch controls the power to the memory array, memory array controller card, and backup device. Moving the Master DC POWER key switch to the ON position will cause the backup SCSI device to spin up. In addition, the front panel POWER lamp will illuminate followed shortly by the STARTUP IN PROGRESS and ACTIVITY indicator lights. The internal backup device will then load the memory arrays with the data stored on the backup device during which time the Excellerator is off-line to the host. When complete, the STARTUP IN PROGRESS and ACTIVITY indicators will go out, indicating that the Solid Data Excellerator has performed initial diagnostics and is ready for use. The Solid Data Excellerator does not require a low-level format. Proper parity is written into the memory array(s) as the unit restores from its backup drive.

NOTE: Turning the Master DC POWER key switch OFF will have no effect on the Excellerators. The unit may be powered down only after a successful shutdown sequence has occurred.
 
 

SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
The following steps should be performed when it is necessary to shutdown the drive.
  1. Backup all data on the Solid State Drive to the operating system. This may take the form of database dumps or UNIX backup utilities such as dump or tar. 
  2. Stop all database servers of other jobs using the Excellerator. 
  3. Dismount the Excellerator from the operating system, if it is mounted. This is done to force the buffer cache to flush.
  4. Unlock the front panel and press the SHUTDOWN switch. The SYSTEM READY lamp will extinguish, the SHUTDOWN IN PROGRESS and ACTIVITY lamps will illuminate, and the Excellerator will start a backup of the memory array(s) to the internal backup drive. This internal backup will take approximately one minute for each 100 MB of installed SDRAM.

    5. ***********************************************************************
    CAUTION: While the Excellerator is backing up to the internal disk, it is off-line to the operating system.
    ***********************************************************************
  5. When the backup has completed, the SHUTDOWN COMPLETE lamp will illuminate. The DC POWER key switch on the rear panel can be turned off. 
STARTUP PROCEDURE
The following steps should be performed when bringing the Excellerator up from a power-off state:
  1. Connect the Excellerator to an AC power source. On the models 800 and 1000, the fans will come on and the batteries will charge.
  2. Turn the DC POWER key switch on the rear panel to the ON position. The front panel POWER lamp will illuminate, followed shortly by the STARTUP IN PROGRESS and ACTIVITY lamps. The Excellerator will restore the contents of the internal backup device to the memory array(s). 

    3. CAUTION: While the Excellerator is restoring data from the internal disk, it is off-line to the operating system.
  3. When the startup operation has completed, the STARTUP IN PROGRESS and ACTIVITY lamps will go out. At this point the Excellerator goes on-line.
  4. Boot the host system, and start the database servers or applications which are utilizing the Excellerator.

6.0 Unique Support Information


 

There are two support models between HP and Solid Data Systems, a Labor-Only Support model and a Full-Support (Parts and Labor) model. There are two variations of the Labor-Only Support model, differentiated by a U.S. agreement and an agreement with Logica-Aldiscon which covers Europe and Asia Pacific.

Labor-Only

For Labor-Only Services for SDS Excellerator products, refer to PSP#10350. 

Full-Support, Worldwide

An agreement between HPCP and SDS reached in 2000, allows HP to provide Full-Support, parts and labor. HP Response Center will take the initial call from end users to diagnose/troubleshoot the Excellerator. HP will log an onsite service request for unit swap if needed. This is the standard multivendor support model.

Additional HPCP/SDS information can be found at: http://hpbrdd32.uksr.hp.com/ Click on Storage, then Solid Data.
